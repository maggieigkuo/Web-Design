From “1950 to 2021 the global birth rate experienced a fall from 5 births per woman to 2.3”

For the last 70 years, fertility rates have decreased worldwide, with a total 50% decline

By 2050, over three-quarters (155 of 204) of countries will not have high enough fertility rates to sustain population size over time

Many countries in sub-Saharan Africa, fertility rates remain high— four children per female in 2021 (nearly 2x the global average)

High-Income Countries Face:

Economic pressures

Shrinking workforce

Responsibility of caring and paying for aging population

Increased cost to raising children

Lower child mortality

Women’s empowerment (education and workforce)

Low-Income Countries Face:

Lack of contraceptions

Lack of healthcare infrastructure

Lack of women’s reproductive rights

Non-prioritization of education and family planning

Climate change effects

line_start THE BOTTOMLINE line_end

Fertility is a global issue — wealthier countries face population shrinkage, while lower-income nations face sustainability challenges.

Empower women — through education, healthcare access, and workplace rights—nations with strong women’s rights are more likely to have better health outcomes and faster economic growth.

A nuanced approach is needed — addressing the fertility crisis requires tailored policies that respect individual choices, promote gender equality, and consider environmental sustainability.
